Over more than 8 years, my research and academic activities have been focused on the modelling of physical properties of solid state materials of interest for technological applications, using simplified statistical models for their description and appropriate analytical and numerical techniques.

My research topics cover a broad range of materials: semiconductors compounds and alloys, topological insulators and unconventional superconductors, with especial focus on technological interest for applications.

Throughout my career, I have demonstrated my ability to undertake new problems and to develop appropriate methodologies in order to achieve the established objectives.

As a computational/theoretical physicist I have gained experience on scientific programming and data-related analysis. I have hands-on experience in: scientific programming and data analysis using C/C++, Fortran and Python, exploratory data analysis and visualization, and machine learning techniques, using Python libraries: NumPy, SciPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Scikit-learn, Tableau, among others. In addition, I have experience working with large scale open-source codes (using MPI parallel computing in a Linux environment) for materials modelling.

I have strong communications skills, having given several invited/contributed talks at national and international meetings/conferences for a wide audience of fellows researchers.
